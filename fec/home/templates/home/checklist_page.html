Congressional candidates are people running for elected office in the Senate or House of Representatives.

Congressional candidates must designate at least one campaign committee. These authorized candidate committees they take in contributions and make expenditures for the candidate’s campaign.

This page is for Congressional candidates and their designated committees.

The checklist below focuses on the mechanics of registration and filing, introducing readers to a few of the most common topics on those subjects. This is neither legal advice nor a comprehensive guide. The FEC recommends all candidates and committees read the campaign guide.

Register with the FEC

Candidates: Register within 15 days of becoming a candidate for federal office. With the exception of “testing the waters” activity, you become a candidate when your campaign contributions or expenditures exceed $5,000.

Candidate committees: Register within 10 days of being officially designated by a candidate on his or her Statement of Candidacy.
