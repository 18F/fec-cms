What does it mean to register and report?

The FEC helps citizens make informed voting decisions based, in part, on knowing the sources of financial support for federal candidates and political committees.

Campaign finance law requires that federal candidates and political committees register with the FEC once they reach certain monetary thresholds or take other actions that indicate they’ll be active in federal elections.

Candidates and candidate committees

Under campaign finance law, a person running for a seat in the House or Senate becomes a candidate when he or she raises or spends more than $5,000 in contributions or expenditures.

Candidates must designate at least one campaign committee. These authorized groups are called candidate committees.
