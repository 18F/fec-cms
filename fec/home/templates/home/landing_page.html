What does it mean to register and report?

The FEC empowers citizens by making campaign finance data available to the general public. Registration and reporting are key components of that process.

Registering with the FEC announces that you’re either a candidate seeking federal office or an individual, committee or organization raising or spending money in federal elections. See a breakdown of these groups on the registration options page.

After registration, FEC filers make regular reports to the FEC or Secretary of Senate. These reports disclose information about their receipts and expenditures. That information is then made available to the public.
